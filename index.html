Djalma Guimarães

Pioneer brazilian geochemist, Professor Emeritus at the Ouro Preto University and Head of Geology of the expedition that discovered the biggest Niobium ore reserve in 1930's in the city of Araxa in Minas Gerais state.

Here is a brief summary of Djalma Guimarães work

Djalma Guimarães was a brazilian scientist, pioneer in the study of the Earth and Geosciences that made an important discovery of mineral resources in the state of Minas Gerais in southeastern region of Brazil.

During his career, he explored and analysed many samples of niobium and even discovered new minerals. Throughtout the direction of the ITI (Industry Technology Institute in Brazil), he created a laboratory for spectrochemical analysis of minerals samples.

Djalma Guimarães was the Head of Geology, working for the Minas Gerais State Government when the biggest niobium ore reserve was discovered in the south of the state, taking Brazil to be the biggest supplier of niobium since 1930's and it still is the largest niobium ore reserve in the entire world.

He published many cientific articles and books internacionaly and his theory of metallogenetics was accepted and included in many Geology Universities in the world, even on the United States.
